# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Gift and treat-yourself shoppers. People buying a handmade forever bouquet as a
present for someone, or as a small personal luxury for themselves. They arrive
casually, often from Instagram, browsing on a phone as much as a desktop. They
are deciding one thing: does this feel special enough to give (or to keep)?
They are not comparison-shopping on price; they are looking for something that
feels considered and made by a person.

Wedding, event, and DIY-kit customers also pass through, but the site is tuned
first for the gift shopper.

## Product Purpose

soft florals sells handmade pipe cleaner "forever flowers": bouquets, frames,
bud vases, gifts, and DIY kits, twisted and styled by hand in a Melbourne
studio. They never wilt and are allergy-free.

The site is a real shop, not a brochure: catalogue, product pages, cart, and a
Stripe checkout. Success is a completed online sale. Everything on the site
exists to move a casual browser to a confident purchase, without ever feeling
pushy.

## Brand Personality

Soft, playful, handmade. Warm and personal, a little whimsical, with a clean
modern finish. The voice is friendly and unhurried, like the maker talking to
you directly, never salesy and never corporate. Craft-fair charm, boutique
execution.

Emotional goals: delight, warmth, calm confidence. A visitor should feel they
have found something small and lovely, and that buying it is easy and safe.

## Anti-references

- **Not corporate / SaaS.** No cold gradients, no hero-metric stat blocks, no
  generic startup polish, no stock-photo gloss. This is not a tech company.
- **Not cutesy-cluttered.** No busy florals tiled everywhere, no sticker
  overload, no comic-energy type, no loud discount-store look. Playful is
  earned through a few good details, not by piling them on.

## Design Principles

1. **The flowers do the selling.** Photography and product are the loudest
   thing on any screen. Navigation, chrome, and copy stay quiet so the work can
   carry the page.
2. **Handmade, not mass-made.** Every surface should feel touched by a person:
   slightly irregular rhythm, warm tone, human copy. Avoid the flat uniformity
   of a templated store.
3. **Gift-worthy.** The site should make a $68 bouquet feel like a thoughtful
   present. Premium but warm; considered, never cheap, never cold.
4. **One calm path from browsing to buying.** Drive the sale, but without
   pressure or dark patterns. Cart and checkout should feel as soft and
   trustworthy as the rest of the shop.
5. **Restraint over decoration.** Whitespace and a few deliberate details beat
   many. When in doubt, remove.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA: sufficient color contrast (watch the pale-pink-on-white
risk), full keyboard navigation, visible focus states, honored
`prefers-reduced-motion`, and meaningful alt text on all product imagery.
